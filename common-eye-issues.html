Myopia (Nearsightedness)

A condition where distant objects appear blurry while close objects can be seen clearly. This occurs when the eyeball is too long or the cornea is too curved, causing light to focus in front of the retina instead of on it.

Hyperopia (Farsightedness)

The opposite of myopia, where close objects appear blurry while distant objects are clear. This happens when the eyeball is too short or the cornea is too flat, causing light to focus behind the retina.

Astigmatism

An irregular curvature of the cornea or lens that causes blurred or distorted vision at all distances. It often occurs alongside myopia or hyperopia.

Read more Simulate

Presbyopia

An age-related condition where the eye's lens becomes less flexible, making it difficult to focus on close objects. It typically begins around age 40 and is a natural part of aging.

Read more Simulate

Glaucoma

A group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often due to high pressure in the eye. It can lead to vision loss and blindness if not treated. Early stages often have no symptoms.

Read more Simulate

Cataracts

A clouding of the eye's natural lens, leading to blurry vision, faded colors, and increased sensitivity to glare. It's common in older adults and can be treated with surgery.

Read more Simulate

Macular Degeneration

An age-related condition affecting the macula (central part of the retina), leading to loss of central vision. It can make it difficult to read, drive, or recognize faces.

Read more Simulate

Diabetic Retinopathy

A complication of diabetes that affects the blood vessels in the retina. It can cause vision loss and blindness if not managed properly.

Read more Simulate

Dry Eye Syndrome

A condition where the eyes don't produce enough tears or the tears evaporate too quickly. It can cause discomfort, irritation, and sometimes blurred vision.

Read more Simulate

Color Blindness

A condition where people have difficulty distinguishing between certain colors, most commonly red and green. It's usually inherited and more common in males.
